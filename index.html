Abstract

We study whether a Vision-Language-Action (VLA) model pre-trained on a bimanual pot-lift task family, fine-tuned with reinforcement learning, can outperform RL-from-scratch MLP policies drawn from the three canonical RL algorithm families (on-policy policy-gradient, off-policy Q-learning, model-based) within a constrained compute budget. We train six agents: a random baseline; PPO, SAC, and a Dyna-style MBPO each from scratch; OpenVLA-OFT fine-tuned with GRPO under the default sparse reward; and the same VLA+GRPO system under an SE(3)/SO(3)-geodesic dense reward we design. Under ~24 GPU-hours of total compute, VLA+GRPO doubles the zero-shot success rate (6.25% → 12.5%) while all three from-scratch MLPs fail to sustain any nonzero held-out success. The SE(3) reward (Modification 2) matches the peak eval success of the default reward but produces a stronger training-rollout signal (12.5% vs. 5.5%), suggesting dense SE(3) shaping works as designed even though the held-out peak was not lifted further within our budget.

Environments

We use two structurally similar bimanual pot-lift environments. Both have a 200-step horizon, a fixed 16-seed held-out eval pool, and the same task-success predicate (grip both handles, lift ≥ 10 cm, keep upright within ±20°), so success_once is directly comparable across them; returns are not.

  • VLA track — RoboTwin lift_pot. Sapien physics, PIPER bimanual (two 7-DOF arms, 14-DOF action space), RGB images plus proprioception, sparse 0/1 reward scaled by reward_coef=5. Chosen because RLinf publish an OpenVLA-OFT SFT checkpoint for this exact task, anchoring the VLA-fine-tuning experiments.
  • MLP-from-scratch track — RoboEval LiftPot [1]. MuJoCo [2] physics, BimanualPanda (two 7-DOF arms + grippers, 16-DOF action space), state-only proprioception (no pixels), dense shaped reward. Chosen because RLinf's MLP training pipeline was already wired up for this env after multiple RoboTwin from-scratch attempts hit a persistent ActorGroup deadlock.

The cross-environment caveat is acknowledged in the analysis: returns cannot be compared, but the success predicate can, and the from-scratch policies score identically zero on RoboEval, which upper-bounds what they would achieve on the structurally similar RoboTwin task within the same compute.

Reward Functions

Three reward formulations are used across the six agents.

RoboEval dense shaped reward (random, B1, B2, B3)

$$r_t = -w_{\text{reach}}\!\left(d_t^{\text{left}} + d_t^{\text{right}}\right) + w_{\text{grasp}} \cdot p_t + w_{\text{lift}} \max(0, \Delta h_t) - w_{\text{pose}} \cdot e_t^{\text{pose}} + w_{\text{success}} \mathbf{1}[\text{success}] - w_{\text{AR}} \|a_t - a_{t-1}\|_2^2$$

Standard shaped reward with reach, subtask, lift, pose, success, and action-rate terms. Default weights $w_{\text{reach}}{=}1.0$, $w_{\text{grasp}}{=}2.0$, $w_{\text{lift}}{=}5.0$, $w_{\text{pose}}{=}0.5$, $w_{\text{success}}{=}10.0$, $w_{\text{AR}}{=}0.1$.

RoboTwin default sparse reward (B4 zero-shot, M1)

$$r_t = 5 \cdot \mathbf{1}[\text{task success predicate at step } t]$$

Zero everywhere except success; return accumulates only after the policy first solves the task.

SE(3)/SO(3)-geodesic dense reward (M2b, ours)

$$\begin{aligned} r_t^{\text{SE(3)}} = \; & -w_p \|p_{\text{pot}} - p_{\text{target}}\|_2 - w_R\, d_{SO(3)}(R_{\text{pot}}, R_{\text{target}}) - w_{ga}\!\!\sum_{s \in \{L, R\}} \|\log_{SE(3)}(H_s^{-1} G_s)\|_2 \\ & + w_{\text{lift}}\max(0, h_t) + w_{\text{grasp}} \cdot \text{subtask}(t) + w_{\text{success}} \mathbf{1}[\text{success}] - w_{\text{smooth}} \|a_t - a_{t-1}\|_2^2 \end{aligned}$$

Coordinate-free dense reward replacing the ad hoc scalar pose term with geodesic distances on the rotation and rigid-body groups, following the geometric control framework of Bullo & Murray [9]. The SO(3) term is the minimum rotation angle aligning $R_{\text{pot}}$ with $R_{\text{target}}$; the gripper-handle term uses the SE(3) twist norm of the relative transform between each gripper frame $G_s$ and target handle frame $H_s$. The subtask term is a four-step staircase: $0.5$ per gripper grasp plus $0.25$ once the pot is lifted > 5 cm. Avoids gimbal lock and generalizes to non-upright targets. Implemented in rlinf/envs/robotwin/lift_pot_reward_wrapper.py with a 15/15-passing unit-test suite for the SE(3)/SO(3) primitives. Default weights $w_p{=}1.0, w_R{=}0.3, w_{ga}{=}0.5, w_{\text{lift}}{=}5.0, w_{\text{grasp}}{=}2.0, w_{\text{success}}{=}10.0, w_{\text{smooth}}{=}0.1$.

Method

We train six agents on a ~24 GPU-hour total budget (PSC Bridges-2, 2× H100 Grace-Hopper). One untrained reference, three RL-from-scratch MLP policies (one per major algorithm family), and two VLA+RL modifications. A seventh "agent" (B4 OpenVLA-OFT zero-shot) is included as a no-RL anchor for the VLA track.

  • Random — uniform random actions on RoboEval. Lower bound on the dense return.
  • B1 / MLP-PPO from scratch [5]. RLinf actor, 2-layer 256-unit MLP, lr=3e-4, clip=0.2, GAE-λ=0.95, 32 envs × 128-step rollouts.
  • B2 / MLP-SAC from scratch. RLinf actor, same MLP head, lr=3e-4, τ=0.005, automatic entropy tuning, 200K-transition replay buffer.
  • B3 / Dyna-1 MBPO from scratch. Standalone PyTorch loop (RLinf does not expose a model-based worker): deterministic MLP dynamics model, SAC actor/critic, 50/50 mix of real and one-step synthetic transitions. No ensemble, no uncertainty-weighted rollouts.
  • B4 / OpenVLA-OFT zero-shot [4]. The published RLinf-OpenVLAOFT-RoboTwin-SFT-lift_pot 7B checkpoint, evaluated without any RL fine-tuning. Reference anchor for the VLA track.
  • M1 / VLA + GRPO [6], default sparse reward. Fine-tunes the SFT checkpoint with Group-Relative Policy Optimization on RLinf [3]: global_batch=32, group_size=4, lr=5e-5 (square-root-scaled from RLinf's published 2e-4 at our 32× smaller global batch), kl_coef=0, FSDP across 2× H100, 6 GRPO epochs (wall-clock cap).
  • M2b / VLA + GRPO + SE(3) reward. Identical setup to M1; only the reward differs (SE(3)/SO(3) dense, see above). Pose/gripper fields needed for the reward are populated inline in RLinf's RoboTwin wrapper since Sapien's subprocess-vectorized envs do not accept gym.Wrapper composition cleanly.

Results

Headline numbers under the mean-of-last-5 held-out eval convention. The OpenVLA-OFT zero-shot reference (B4) attains 6.25% without any RL, which is what the M1/M2b 12.5% peaks improve over (a 2× lift on top of the pretrained VLA).

Takeaways

  • VLA pretraining is the load-bearing ingredient in this regime. ~7 GPU-hours of GRPO fine-tuning on top of OpenVLA-OFT moves zero-shot 6.25% to 12.5%; ~12 GPU-hours of from-scratch RL across three algorithm families (on-policy PG, off-policy Q-learning, model-based) sustains 0%.
  • SE(3) reward is a null result on eval, mild positive on training rollouts. Peak eval ties with the default reward (12.5% vs 12.5%), but M2b is the only VLA+RL run whose training-rollout success also reaches 12.5% (vs M1's 5.5%), and its training diagnostics are healthier (clip_fraction 0.32 at epoch 3 vs M1's 0.57). The denser signal works as designed; held-out generalization is bottlenecked elsewhere — most likely GRPO group sparsity on a 3.13% SFT, no entropy regularization, and seed distribution drift between train and eval.
  • MBPO inaction pathology. B3's policy converges to near-zero actions: the dense reward's penalty terms are minimized in the "do nothing" basin where the learned dynamics model is most accurate. Classic Dyna pathology that a full MBPO with ensemble dynamics and uncertainty-aware rollouts would likely avoid.
